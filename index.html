Sample Note 1: Low MDM 99308 - Low Complexity
SUBJECTIVE
• Patient resting comfortably in bed • No complaints of pain or distress • Tolerating current vent settings well • Family reports patient has been stable
OBJECTIVE
VITALS: T 98.6°F, BP 128/78, HR 78, RR 14, SpO2 96% PHYSICAL EXAM: • General: Alert, comfortable, non-distressed • HEENT: Normocephalic, atraumatic, pupils equal and reactive • Cardiovascular: Regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs • Pulmonary: Clear breath sounds bilaterally, ventilator dependent • Abdomen: Soft, non-tender, non-distended • Extremities: No edema, pulses 2+ throughout • Skin: Intact, no rashes or lesions • Neurological: Alert and oriented x3
ASSESSMENT
• COPD - Stable on current vent settings • Dry eyes - Controlled with artificial tears • Vitamin D deficiency - On supplementation • Benign prostate hyperplasia - Stable with indwelling catheter
PLAN
• Continue current vent settings and weaning protocol • Continue scheduled nebulizer treatments • Continue current medication regimen • Routine nursing care and monitoring • Foley catheter care per protocol
Sample Note 2: Moderate MDM 99309 - Moderate Complexity
SUBJECTIVE
• Patient reports increased cough with yellow sputum production • Mild shortness of breath during weaning trials • Tolerating tube feeds without issues • Family concerned about increased respiratory symptoms
OBJECTIVE
VITALS: T 100.2°F, BP 142/88, HR 92, RR 18, SpO2 92% PHYSICAL EXAM: • General: Mild respiratory distress, diaphoretic • HEENT: Normocephalic, atraumatic, pupils equal and reactive • Cardiovascular: Tachycardic, regular rhythm, no murmurs • Pulmonary: Coarse breath sounds with expiratory wheezing bilaterally • Abdomen: Soft, non-tender, bowel sounds active • Extremities: 1+ pedal edema, no cyanosis • Skin: Stage 4 sacral ulcer 4x3cm with 80% granulation tissue • Neurological: Alert and oriented x3
ASSESSMENT
• COPD with acute bronchitis exacerbation • Normocytic anemia - status post transfusion, monitoring • Decubitus ulcer stage 4 - improving with wound care • UTI - resolved after antibiotic course • Hypertension - controlled on current medications
PLAN
• Continue vent weaning with close monitoring of oxygen saturation • Add bronchodilator nebulizers every 6 hours • Monitor anemia symptoms, repeat CBC in 1 week • Continue aggressive wound care with turning schedule every 2 hours • Continue current medication regimen • Monitor for fever progression or respiratory deterioration
Sample Note 3: High MDM 99310 - High Complexity
SUBJECTIVE
• Patient found lethargic with increased respiratory distress • Family reports decreased responsiveness throughout the day • Increased vent requirements noted overnight • Decreased urine output reported by nursing
OBJECTIVE
VITALS: T 102.1°F, BP 84/52, HR 128, RR 22, SpO2 88% PHYSICAL EXAM: • General: Lethargic, diaphoretic, in moderate respiratory distress • HEENT: Pale conjunctivae, dry mucous membranes • Cardiovascular: Tachycardic, faint heart sounds, jugular venous distension 8cm • Pulmonary: Increased work of breathing, coarse crackles throughout lung fields • Abdomen: Soft, mildly distended, bowel sounds diminished • Extremities: Cool and clammy, 2+ pitting edema to mid-calf • Skin: Diaphoretic, stage 4 sacral ulcer 6x5cm with purulent drainage • Neurological: Lethargic but arousable, oriented to person only
ASSESSMENT
• Sepsis with respiratory failure secondary to healthcare-associated pneumonia • Acute kidney injury likely prerenal and septic • ANCA vasculitis flare in setting of infection • COPD exacerbation with acute respiratory failure • Hypotensive shock requiring pressor support • Infected decubitus ulcer contributing to sepsis
PLAN
• Increase vent support for respiratory failure with goal SpO2 >92% • Initiate vancomycin and Zosyn for broad-spectrum coverage of healthcare-associated pneumonia • IV fluid resuscitation with normal saline bolus and vasopressor protocol for septic shock • Nephrology consultation for acute kidney injury management • Infectious disease consultation for antibiotic stewardship • Surgical consultation for wound evaluation and possible debridement • Frequent vital sign monitoring every 15 minutes until stable • Repeat labs in 4 hours including lactate, CBC, and chemistry panel • Stress dose steroids for vasculitis flare and adrenal insufficiency coverage • Continue all supportive care measures including turning, skin care, and pulmonary toilet

Key Documentation Differences

For 99308 (Low MDM) Documentation:

  • Focus on stability assessments
  • Routine monitoring parameters
  • Continuation of established treatments
  • Minimal data review documentation

For 99309 (Moderate MDM) Documentation:

  • Specific symptoms and changes documented
  • Abnormal findings with clinical context
  • Treatment adjustments with rationale
  • Limited data review (1-2 test results)

For 99310 (High MDM) Documentation:

  • Critical abnormalities highlighted prominently
  • Multiple interacting problems described
  • Complex management decisions with specialist input
  • Extensive data review and analysis documented
